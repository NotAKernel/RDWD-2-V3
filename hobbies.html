My Hobbies

Literature

Reading & Writing

I quite enjoy writing in general - sometimes it is poetry and other times, more often than the former, it is prose. I have been writing ever since I can remember, and I still have a few of those stories lying around here and there. It is a dream of mine to get a book published, and possibly have that book become well-known; I have been working towards this dream for a few years now, thought it must be said with no lack of redrafts and complete scraps.

Naturally, I like to read. As of writing this, I am almost the whole way through the complete Sherlock Holmes collection, which I have read parts of now and again but finally decided to read the stories in their entirety. Reading is also one of the earliest memories of mine, and I started with some girly teenage books on my sister's shelf. Since then, I've transitioned more into reading classics - still, I have those first books to thank for inspiring my love of reading, which in turn inspires me when I write. I suppose the title of 'favourite' will have to go to The Count of Monte Cristo by Alexandre Dumas; it is an excellent mesh of interwoven tales and backstories, with the best undertones of revenge that I have yet experienced. My utmost recommendation goes without saying.

Music

Listening and Playing

I listen to music practically everyday, mostly when I'm doing some kind of work or simply to relax (right now I'm listening to Days of Future Passed by The Moody Blues). Not much to be said about this one, as I think everyone understands why music is universally cherished, even those peculiar ones that don't enjoy it. The main bands I listen to are anywhere from the 60s to the 80s, with a few modern ones too. Electric Light Orchestra first introduced me to listening to full albums and hearing the different sounds of an artist/band as they progress. For that reason, they'll always be my number one band, but bands like Supertramp, The Alan Parson Project and The Moody Blues aren't far behind.

The instruments I play are piano and guitar, though I've been playing piano for a lot longer - somewhere around ten years or so. My mother teaches piano, so that isn't so surprising. I mostly taught myself, and so the theory side of things has suffered a bit and I pick pieces up faster by ear than by sight-reading. It's a great thing to be able to play an instrument - a form of therapy, I find.

Games

Board Games to Video Games

Arguably, one of my favourite ways to pass time. It's much more fun with friends, but most of these can also be played alone if there's no one around. I quite enjoy playing chess and learning different openings, but I also like to solve chess puzzles and see a powerful position or trade unravel itself on the board.

As for video games, I've played a lot. Most of the time I spend playing games is with friends, however a good single-player game with a compelling story and good mechanics is right up my alley. One I'm playing at the moment is Dead Cells, which is a roguelite. At first glance, doing the same thing over and over again in different ways may seem a quick way to get bored, but the gameplay mechanics are what keep me playing it. The art style is pretty good, too.

Daniel Christian Yermak
